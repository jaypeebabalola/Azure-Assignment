EMPLOYMENT TO POPULATION RATIO BY BABALOLA PRECIOUS-18CJ024111

Definition: Employment to population ratio is the proportion of a country's population that is employed. Employment is defined as persons of working age who, during a short reference period, were engaged in any activity to produce goods or provide services for pay or profit, whether at work during the reference period (i.e. who worked in a job for at least one hour) or not at work due to temporary absence from a job, or to working-time arrangements. Ages 15-24 are generally considered the youth population. Source: International Labour Organization, ILOSTAT database. Data retrieved in September 2019.
